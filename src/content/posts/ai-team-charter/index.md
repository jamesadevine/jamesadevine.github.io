AI can accelerate a team, but only if we are deliberate about how we use it. This charter sets a shared standard for quality, accountability, and trust.

## AI Team Charter

1. **Use AI to affirm understanding**
   - Use AI to test your understanding, not replace it.
   - If AI output conflicts with what you have learned, pause and verify against reliable sources (official docs, code, data, or subject matter experts).
   - Treat contradictions as a signal to investigate, not as proof either side is correct.

2. **Lead with human expertise**
   - If a team member asks you a question, answer from your own knowledge first.
   - Use AI to double-check details when needed, especially for unfamiliar or high-risk topics.
   - People ask colleagues for judgment and context; AI is support, not substitution.

3. **Verify before you share**
   - Do not forward AI answers without checking facts, assumptions, and references.
   - For important decisions, cite the source of truth used for validation.
   - If confidence is low, say so clearly.

4. **Make decisions traceable**
   - Record where AI contributed to decisions, code, content, or analysis.
   - Keep prompts, outputs, and key edits where appropriate so others can review the reasoning.
   - Ensure work remains understandable without rerunning the AI conversation.

5. **Protect security, privacy, and IP**
   - Never paste secrets, sensitive data, or confidential material into tools that are not approved for that data.
   - Follow company policy on data handling, retention, and model usage.
   - When unsure, default to caution and escalate.

6. **Set quality bars for AI-assisted work**
   - AI-generated work must meet the same standards as human-authored work.
   - Test, review, and validate outputs before merge or publication.
   - “AI suggested it” is never an acceptable reason to skip quality checks.

7. **Use AI where it adds the most value**
   - Prioritize repetitive drafting, summarization, exploration, and scaffolding tasks.
   - Keep high-impact judgment calls with accountable humans.
   - Regularly review where AI is helping versus creating rework.

8. **Continuously improve team practice**
   - Share strong prompts, failed prompts, and lessons learned.
   - Review this charter periodically and update it as tools and risks evolve.
   - Build AI literacy across the team so quality improves over time.

---

Use AI to go faster, but use human judgment to go in the right direction.
